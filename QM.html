839N1: Quantitative methods for STI studies


Jointly with R Camerani (Lecturer) and Y Aslan (Tutor). 2020-2021 (spring).

The module provides students with a training in quantitative methods and is offered to students of various backgrounds who already have some knowledge in basic statistics. The emphasis lies squarely on two aspects: the range of possible techniques and their appropriateness for particular circumstances, and secondly their applicability to standard problems arising in everyday research. The module covers widely used methods and will also provide students with an understanding of the main issues that can arise in applied quantitative studies.

The module equips students with methodological skills to read, interpret and produce quantitative research both within and outside academia (e.g. consultancy reports, policy-evaluation documents). The module is organised in weekly lectures and workshops. The lectures provide an introduction to the theoretical and -in particular- practical elements of each method. The workshops are hands-on sessions, in which students are guided through a set of practical exercises on the widely used statistical software STATA.
